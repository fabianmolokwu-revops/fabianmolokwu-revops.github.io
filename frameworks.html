// FRAMEWORKS

Mental models I use to think about revenue automation, lead qualification, and funnel architecture. These are established industry frameworks — not something I invented. They guide how I build automations and structure data.

4Qs Lead Qualification

Popularized in B2B RevOps circles

Quantity, Quality, Quickness, Query — a filter for separating signal from noise in inbound leads. I apply this when designing lead routing rules and CRM validation workflows. For example: automate lead scoring so only human, high-intent leads reach the sales team.

PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)

Classic copywriting psychology framework

Identify the problem, agitate the cost of inaction, present the solution. I use this structure when writing email sequences for Klaviyo flows — especially Welcome and Abandoned Cart recovery emails.

BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

Customer journey mapping methodology

Map the customer's current state (Before), their desired state (After), and the Bridge (your solution). I use this when designing multi-step email flows and automation triggers — what does the customer need to see at each stage?

AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

Established marketing funnel model

Engineer high-intent engagement through cognitive layers. I apply AIDA when structuring landing pages, email sequences, and conversion paths — making sure each step builds toward the next action.

GEO / AEO (Generative & Answer Engine Optimization)

Emerging discipline for AI search

As AI agents become the primary discovery layer, content must be structured for LLM readability. I apply schema markup (JSON-LD), clear heading hierarchies, and authoritative on-page signals so that my work — and my clients' content — can be accurately cited by AI search engines.

72-Hour Nurture Logic

Lead response time research

Data shows lead engagement drops dramatically after 72 hours. I use this principle when configuring automation triggers: immediate follow-up (Hour 0-4), authority-building content (Hour 24), and a clear call-to-action (Hour 72). Applied in Make.com scenarios and Klaviyo flows.

Demand Unit Waterfall

SiriusDecisions / Forrester

A framework for measuring every stage of the funnel — from total addressable market to closed-won revenue. I use this logic when building HubSpot/Salesforce dashboards and Looker Studio reports: what are we measuring, and where is the drop-off?

ABM Tiered Orchestration

Account-Based Marketing best practices

Personalize outreach at scale across 1:1, 1:few, and 1:many tiers. I apply this when designing lead routing rules and segmentation logic in CRMs — different accounts get different treatment based on priority.
